Happy Birthday!

Turning 21 aunt, MashaAllah. The moment we first talked on the 27th of July till 7 in the morning, it's crazy, but I knew we were going to have history together. I still had the better sunset photo though. I still remember wanting to meet you for the very first time. I was so nervous, and I still remember it vividly: the very first time I laid my eyes on you, wearing the black kameez that I loved seeing you in, and all that "ghussa" evaporating from your face the moment you saw me, turning into a shy blob.

Starting that chapter with you was a really important phase of my life, where you changed my perspective and priorities and made me turn into a better man because I realized that's what you deserve, because that's how you made me feel. You made me want to be a better man for you from the love and happiness you gave. I remember the bicep pics and corny-ass jokes when we were in our limerence phase, and the connection we built in an instant like it was yesterday. Sometimes I ponder truly what a small world it is that I got to know you, and how Allah had written our story.

You have always been a rock, someone so loving, kind, a ray of sunshine that seeps through the clouds, a star that shines extra bright in the night sky, someone who sticks out in a crowd like others are just extras in a movie scene. You have always stuck out to me, ever since I've gotten to know you. Your frizzy hair, your eyebrows, your nose, your smile, your teeth, your Gajni memory, your hands, me touching your nails, and most importantly, that fuckass personality of yours. Your care and love for the people who are actually close to you, which I've experienced firsthand has all been etched into my brain. Your beauty is truly not made for eyes of this world. Seeing you love truly, purely, and wholeheartedly has inspired me to love like that too.

Seeing the spark in your eyes when you talk about your aspirations and dreams is so pure, and InshaAllah you're going to achieve all of them. You're going to continue to love animals, travel around the world, and live alone, just like you wanted.

I just want to appreciate the love and the feeling of being seen and chosen that you provided me. You made me feel all those things on my last birthday, and I truly appreciate you for that. Thank you for all the memories we've had and all the emotions we've shared together, for all the songs and the movies we've shared together (yes, Tangled counts), and to the songs and movies we wanted to share together but won't.

I could write a novel about you, but in the end, all I want to say is: it was a good chapter, even if the ending was long overdue.I'm over the reality of us, but sometimes I still miss the idea of you and that you're truly an amazing, incredible, mature, stubborn, fatass woman. I truly wish you extreme happiness, love, and health wherever you are in your life. I wish and hope you have an incredible 21st birthday.
From Musab.
